# Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 14, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how seeker alert ("the app", "we", "our", or "us") may collect, process, and use information when the app, backend services, or related repository materials are used.

## 1. Overview

seeker alert is designed to minimize unnecessary data collection.
The app is intended to monitor Solana-related alert conditions such as token price changes and wallet activity, and to deliver notifications to users.

## 2. Information We May Process

Depending on how seeker alert is deployed and used, we may process limited information including:

- Public wallet addresses used for authentication or alert ownership
- User-created alert rules and related configuration
- Alert trigger history and notification records
- Technical logs needed for backend stability and debugging
- Device, app version, crash, or diagnostics information
- Token contract addresses or on-chain identifiers submitted for alert tracking

## 3. Information We Do Not Intend to Collect

seeker alert is not designed to require users to submit highly sensitive wallet secrets such as:

- seed phrases
- secret recovery phrases
- private keys

We also do not intentionally request direct personal identity information such as:

- legal name
- home address
- government ID numbers
- phone number

## 4. How Information May Be Used

Any limited information processed by seeker alert may be used to:

- authenticate a wallet session
- create and manage alerts
- deliver notifications and alert history
- operate and secure backend systems
- diagnose bugs, crashes, and service issues
- improve app performance and usability

## 5. Third-Party Services

seeker alert may rely on third-party infrastructure or APIs, which may independently process technical information.
Examples may include:

- Solana RPC providers
- Firebase
- Jupiter APIs
- wallet SDKs and mobile wallet adapters
- analytics or crash-reporting tools

Those services are governed by their own privacy policies and terms.

## 6. Data Storage and Retention

How long data is stored depends on how the app is deployed.
A self-hosted or forked deployment may store logs, alerts, or event history differently from another deployment.
If you deploy your own version of seeker alert, you are responsible for your own retention, deletion, and security practices.

## 7. Data Security

We take reasonable measures to reduce unnecessary data exposure.
However, no mobile app, backend service, repository, or cloud platform can guarantee absolute security.
Anyone operating seeker alert is responsible for securing:

- backend credentials
- cloud accounts
- API keys and service secrets
- Android signing keys
- production infrastructure and logs

## 8. Children's Privacy

seeker alert is not specifically directed to children under the age of 13.
We do not knowingly design the app to collect personal information from children.

## 9. Open-Source Repository Scope

This repository is published as an open-source project and is sanitized for sharing.
It does not include private deployment secrets, release keystores, local environment files, or private runtime data.

## 10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time.
Any updates may be published in the repository, app distribution materials, or related project pages.

## 11. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact the project maintainer through the repository on GitHub.
